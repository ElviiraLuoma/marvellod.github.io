Introduction about the project

Our project has at its core the long and prolific collaboration between the author Roald Dahl and the illustrator Quentin Blake. Their meeting in an English publishing house in 1976 marked the beginning of a path destined to change the history of children's literature. How many of us could ever forget the disarming courage of Matilda or the sincere kindness of the BFG? And how many, as soon as these titles are pronounced, do not have in their minds the image of the marvellous illustrations that are so wonderful for their communicative simplicity?

We first selected 10 objects that exemplified their relationship and that came from a context of Libraries, Archives or Museums. In choosing the objects we have tried to emphasize the indispensability of the illustrative aspect. Then we represented them through an E/R Model describing our idea and highlighting the relationship between our entities. We decided to put the general entity of the relationship between Roald Dahl and Quentin Blake at the centre of our model. The reason for this choice stemmed from the desire to focus on their collaboration “in fieri”: the 10 objects are the emblem of this relationship, the product of which has had an extraordinary impact in the culture not only in Europe but worldwide. The next step was to create a Metadata Standard Alignment for each object, including the different standards capable of describing the relative information. Then, we developed a theoretical model asking ourselves fundamental questions about our objects: Who? When? What? and Where? This section was important to validate the effectiveness of our model.

In the conceptual model, we re-formulated the abovementioned questions through existing schemes, vocabularies and ontologies. The priority was to emphasize the relationships between concepts. Based on these results, we dedicated ourselves to the description of data. Among all the descriptions selected three particularly significant entities which have served as an example for the description of our data in RDF language. Finally, after having formally structured our data, we focused on the possible interconnections between our data and with the controlled authorities present on the web.

We tackled this specific theme to underline the importance of children's literature in general while its products too often fail to find a prominent place in the cultural heritage domain. Entering the heart of the matter, we have tried to emphasize the importance of the illustrative aspect, too often left in the background. We firmly believe that the process we developed and exemplified in the specific Dahl-Blake relationship can be re-applied in multiple contexts related to children's literature. Indeed, innumerable cornerstones of both European and World literature are based on the close collaboration between an author and an illustrator.
